About

My Story

Art has always existed in my life in many capacities. It has been my relief, my greatest struggle, my best friend, my therapist and now my official partner in crime. I originally planned to go to school for illustration seeing as I could focus on any task in school until the entire sidebar of my page was filled with countless doodles. It wasn’t until high-school that I realized that I thought far more analytically than the realm of traditional art demands and that is when I transitioned into the wonderous world of Graphic Design.

I pursued Graphic Design through Niagara College for a 3 year program and absolutely fell in love with the skill. I was whole-heartedly invested in learning as much as I could digest and creating as much as I could conceptualize. I graduated from my program with Presidential Honours and a full time job as a Junior Graphic Designer. I worked at that job for months utilizing the best learning tool for any human; real life experience. I continued to do the work but slowly the excitement of just landing a job faded and I was left with a job that didn’t provide any fulfillment. So I decided to open myself to the world of Freelance Design.

Thus the birth of my current standing design title: “Oddish Studio”. I chose Oddish because I have always looked at the world through a very unique lens. I apprecaited the quirks, glitches, unanswerable, odd parts of life. I was drawn to them. I enjoy the risk and adrenaline of not walking along the beaten path and I wanted to hone that in my career. Freelancing was one of the most difficult but rewarding jobs I have ever had.

Throughout all of my education I was fascinated with web design; the layouts, interactivty, user based content and sheer complication amazed me. That’s when I found myself searching for any way to learn more about UI and Web design as I possibly could until I stumbled upon CareerFoundry’s UI Course. I immediately enrolled and fell even more in love with this industry and began the cycle of learning and creating once more. I have used this time and effort within learning institutions and out in the real world to better myself as a designer and person every single day. Not a day goes by where I don’t appreciate the journey I have taken or look forward in excitement for what the industry and my future may have in store. I couldn’t be more excited to continue.
